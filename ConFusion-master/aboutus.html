About Us

Our Story


Men dominate most computer science classes, and sometimes create a negative learning environment for the women in the class. Most people believe that women are not suited to be in the computer science field. However, this assumption is wrong and sexist. Women have just as much of an equal right as men do. There should be no difference in treatment.

Our goal is to provide the statistical data about gender diversity in computer science department and classrooms at various universities. We also want to provide a safe environment for women to be able to voice their opinions about the computer science departments at their university. Women can also rate their computer science department so other college students or high school students can determine if the university has diverse computer science department.

Inspiration

Ellen Pao Investment Partner, Chief Diversity and Inclusion Officer, Co-Founder and CEO of Project Include

Ellen Pao was born to immigrant chinese parents she grew up in new jersey. Pao received a degree in electrical engineering from Princeton in 1991, a law degree from Harvard in 1994, and a MBA from Harvard in 1998. She worked at several companies in Silicon Valley, in 2005 pao worked for Kleiner Perkins as a chief of staff for co-found John Doerr. However in 2012 pao filed a gender discrimination lawsuit against er employer because, after filing the lawsuit she was fired without warning and while Kleiner Perkins said she was given a month to accept and a paid five month transition. No such thing happened and Pao won the lawsuit. Ever since she has been an advocate for having equal gender right in work environments, especially in the tech industry. She created Project Include with five other women in order to develop better advise for human resources that involve a series of meetings with clients and publishes anonymized progress reports under a Creative Commons license, for startup tech companies.
